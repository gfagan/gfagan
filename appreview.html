Application Review

Note: This project is still ongoing. To comply with my non-disclosure agreement, I have omitted and obfuscated confidential information in this case study and blurred the images where necessary. All information in this case study is my own and does not necessarily reflect the views of Accenture or its clients. .

Our Mission

To carry out a review of an existing Record Management System for a Public Safety Service client, utilising Design Thinking and it’s user centric approach to achieve the following objectives.

  1. To review, analyse, and agree on design priorities for enhancements of the System, to enable a better user experience and address highlighted issues.
  2. To co-create a roadmap of further enhancements and future needs for the Record Management System, and the client’s overall suite of ICT applications.

My Role

I have been the UX Designer on this project, assisting with the Research, Analysis and Workshops, whilst also being solely responsible for the wireframing and prototype deliverables.

Research

We started by carrying out six immersion sessions, where we shadowed over 25 users in their day-to-day use of the system, before following this up with individual user interviews.

For each user, we recorded what they were doing, saying, the pain-points they encountered, and any insights we had. Further to this, we also reviewed the existing collateral and carried out an evaluation of the application's UI.

From all of this, we outlined the following:

  • Three main user groups
  • Seventy five pain-points
  • Five focus areas
  • Seven design priorities

From the user groups, my colleagues created three matching personas. I then set about creating a User Journeys for each one to be demoed back to the user groups as part of their group specific workshops.

Pain-point Evaluation

The pain-points established from our user research helped us identify, from the user’s perspective, where frustrations were being encountered.

The Digital Transformation Consultant on our team carried out a pain-point mapping exercise to firstly validate that our focus areas were correct, and secondly to highlight which areas had the greatest allocation of pain-points.

My colleague also broke the pain-points up into associated categories, such as Functionality, Information Architecture, and Training, providing us with a holistic and more digestible view of the issues at hand.

Workshops

We played back our As-Is findings to each user group in their own specific workshop, discussing with them the five focus area we had identified, and empathising with their user journey and the pain-points that we had recorded.

The Workshops were also used to co-create the To-Be user journeys for each group, were the users helped us map out their ideal scenario with the use of post-it notes.

Prototyping was also carried out by the users, helping us get an idea of how the wished certain flows or screens to be laid out.

As some of the user feedback we had received was in relation to screens being cluttered with non-important information, or mandatory validations being required on unnecessary fields, we carried out a field prioritisation task. This allowed users to rank the fields in terms of importance, and highlight the mandatory fields that they wished to alter.

Prototyping

With all the information we had collected to date, I began prototyping the To-Be application, arranging user feedback sessions along the way in an effort to maintain our co-creation ethos and keeping users as involved in the process as their time allowed.

At this stage we had began to identify a potential requirement to migrate the application from its current technology stack in order to provide the user with the full functionality that they required.

As a result, we identified immediate enhancements that could be made within the existing application framework, forming the basis of one prototype which looked namely at improving the overall Information Architecture of the application, the user flows, and reducing steps were possible within the system’s limitations. This prototype I developed in Axure in high-fidelity, based on the existing User Interface.

I also generated a second prototype, of low to medium fideltiy, to display the future state for a key focus area - Search. Here I looked to maximise our understanding of the issues the users faced, empathising with their need for the following:

  • Simplified Search screen
  • Multiple Tabs
  • Ability to cross reference and multitask.

Roadmap

Finally, we presented back our overall journey and proposed roadmap to the users and key stakeholders. A review of this roadmap is still underway with the client and the project is ongoing.
